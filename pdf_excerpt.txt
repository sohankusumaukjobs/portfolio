Abstract 
Wildfires represent a significant danger to ecosystems, economy, and human safety, with their 
occurrence increasing owing to climate change and anthropogenic activities.  Prompt and 
precise detection is crucial to mitigate damage and facilitate efficient emergency actions.  This 
study presents a deep learning-based wildfire detection system that incorporates sophisticated 
image preprocessing techniques, including Multi-Scale Retinex (MSR) and RGB-to-YCbCr 
color space translation, with the DenseNet121 architecture.  The Wildfire Dataset, consisting 
of various fire and non-fire photos, was utilized for model training and assessment.  
Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) identified significant difficulties including brightness 
fluctuation, class imbalance, and resolution discrepancies.  Results indicate that DenseNet with 
preprocessing attained an impressive 98.84% test accuracy, in contrast to 90.95% without 
preprocessing, reflecting an 8% increase.  The accuracy of the CNN model increased from 
82.89% to 92.11% following preprocessing.  Preprocessing significantly improved contrast, 
feature visibility, and resilience, resulting in substantial decreases in misclassification rates.  
The results confirm that improving input quality by MSR and color space conversion directly 
enhances classification performance, particularly under difficult visual situations like smoke, 
haze, and inadequate illumination.  This study enhances image-based wildfire detection 
capabilities, providing a more precise and dependable solution than conventional raw-image 
classification methods.
